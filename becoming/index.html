1756 - 1762

Becoming a Printer

Isaiah Thomas was born in Boston on January 19, 1749. His father, Moses Thomas, was a restless man who drifted from job to job. He left his family to seek his fortune in the southern colonies and was never heard from again. He died in North Carolina in 1752. This left Isaiah’s mother destitute with five young children to support. Isaiah was placed in what was then a form of welfare. Because his mother could not afford to keep him, the Overseers of the Poor, a governmental agency in Boston, placed him in an apprenticeship with Zechariah Fowle (1724-1776). Isaiah was just seven years old when he entered into this apprenticeship.

According to Thomas, Fowle was not a very successful printer. He published one-page songs called broadside ballads, cheap pamphlets, and other short printed materials. Thomas himself called Fowle “uncommonly ignorant” and was particularly upset that, despite his promises, Fowle did not adequately provide for Isaiah&rsqup;s education. In fact, Thomas received no formal education at all. He learned to read and write by studying an “Ink-stained Bible and Dictionary” in the pressroom and by setting type. Despite this, Thomas was extremely bright and was soon a better printer and businessman than his master.
