Ebenezer Storer

Ebenezer Storer (1729-1807) was a prominent Boston philanthropist and merchant. His business was called Ebenezer Storer and Son. He graduated from Harvard in 1747 and served as that institutionâ€™s treasurer from 1777 until his death. He was active in Boston public life. In addition to serving as an Overseer of the Poor he was also at various times a warden and selectmen. Storer was a founder and the first treasurer of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences as well as a charter member and treasurer for 17 years of the Society for Propagating the Gospel in North America.
